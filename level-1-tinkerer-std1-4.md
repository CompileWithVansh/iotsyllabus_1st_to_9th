# LEVEL 1 — TINKERER ⚡
## Standards 1st–4th | Theme: "Electricity, Motion & Machines — No Computer Needed"
### 36 Sessions × 1 hr | 9 Months

---

## Overview
No Arduino. No coding. Just pure, hands-on electronics and mechanical building. Students discover how electricity works, build real circuits, and construct machines that move — all without a single line of code.

**Sub-level A:** Standards 1–2 (Sessions 1–18)
**Sub-level B:** Standards 3–4 (Sessions 19–36)

---

## Component Inventory

| Category | Components |
|----------|-----------|
| Power | Coin cells (CR2032), AA batteries, 9V batteries, battery holders, battery clips |
| Light | LEDs (red, green, blue, yellow, white), 220Ω resistors |
| Sound | Active buzzers |
| Switches | Push buttons, slide switches, toggle switches |
| Motion | DC motors (small, 3–6V), propeller/fan blades, asymmetric weights (blobs of hot glue/putty) |
| Wiring | Jumper wires, alligator clip wires, copper tape, conductive thread |
| Motor Control | L298N motor driver module |
| Display | 7-segment display (no IC, just LED wiring) |
| Misc | Cardboard, craft materials, toothbrush heads, lightweight styrofoam/card base |

## ═══════════════════════════════════════════
## SUB-LEVEL A — Standards 1st & 2nd (Sessions 1–18)
## Theme: "Electricity Is Everywhere"
## ═══════════════════════════════════════════

### Learning Outcomes (Sub-level A)
By session 18, students can:
- Explain what electricity is and why it flows
- Build a working circuit on a breadboard or with alligator clips
- Use LEDs, buzzers, switches, and a DC motor
- Complete copper tape paper circuits
- Build and present their first solo invention

---

## UNIT 1 — What Is Electricity? (Sessions 1–3)
*"Why does a torch glow but a stone doesn't?"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 1 | Orientation — what is electronics & robotics? | Show finished projects: torch, vibrating bug, mini car. Ask: "Which of these can YOU build?" |
| 2 | Electric charges, current, voltage — in simple words | Rub balloon on hair (static). Coin cell + alligator clip + LED → it glows! |
| 3 | Batteries — types, sizes, voltage | Handle AA, AAA, 9V, coin cell. Which ones work for LEDs? Test each. |

---

## UNIT 2 — LED Circuits (Sessions 4–7)
*"Light it up your way"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 4 | Conductors vs insulators | Touch-test: connect coin cell + LED through different materials (plastic, metal, eraser, foil). Which glows? |
| 5 | First breadboard circuit — LED + battery | Challenge: Light up your first LED on the breadboard! |
| 6 | Series vs parallel circuits | String of LEDs in series vs parallel — what happens when one is removed? |
| 7 | **Challenge: Design a 4-LED pattern** | Build your name initial using LEDs and copper tape on card |

## UNIT 3 — Switches & Control (Sessions 8–10)
*"YOU are in control"*

| 9 | Slide switch — on/off control | Make an LED torch using slide switch + LED + battery holder |
| 10 | **Challenge: Secret Alarm** | Buzzer triggers only when hidden switch is pressed (inside a box) |

## UNIT 5 — DC Motors & Motion (Sessions 13–15)
*"Things that spin and move"*

## UNIT 6 — Vibrating Bug Project (Session 16)
*"Build a creature that MOVES!"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 16 | Vibrating motor + asymmetric weight = vibration | Attach a small blob of putty/glue off-center on motor shaft → it vibrates! Mount on lightweight base with marker-leg bristles or bent wire feet. Decorate as a beetle/bug. Switch on → bug CRAWLS across the table! |

> **WOW factor:** Students decorate their vibrating bug with googly eyes, wings, and antennae. Place all bugs on a surface and race them. No code needed — pure physics!

## ═══════════════════════════════════════════
## SUB-LEVEL B — Standards 3rd & 4th (Sessions 19–36)
## Theme: "Machines We Built Without a Computer"
## ═══════════════════════════════════════════

### Learning Outcomes (Sub-level B)
By session 36, students can:
- Explain series/parallel circuits with confidence
- Understand capacitors and electromagnets at a conceptual level
- Build a button-controlled RC car using L298N + 4 push buttons (no Arduino)
- Construct high-level machines: hovercraft, electromagnetic crane, bristle-bot
- Identify resistors, diodes, and basic electronic components

---

## UNIT 8 — Advanced Circuits (Sessions 19–21)
*"Circuits that do more"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 19 | Series circuit deep dive | Calculate total resistance. Build a 3-LED series chain with resistors — measure brightness |
| 20 | Parallel circuit deep dive | Build parallel LED panel — compare brightness to series. Real-world: house wiring is parallel |
| 21 | **Challenge: Series vs Parallel Race** | Teams build both circuits. Which uses battery faster? Measure with multimeter |

---

## UNIT 9 — New Components (Sessions 22–23)
*"Meet the building blocks of electronics"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 22 | Resistors — color code, values, what they do | Color band decoding activity. Build resistor divider, observe voltage difference |
| 23 | Diodes — one-way current only | Connect LED forward biased vs reverse biased. Observe: only one direction glows |

---

## UNIT 10 — Capacitors & Electromagnets (Sessions 24–26)
*"Store it, release it — magnetism from electricity"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 24 | Capacitor — stores charge, releases it | Charge a capacitor with battery → disconnect → it powers a small LED briefly! |
| 25 | Capacitor flash LED | Charge-release cycle: LED flashes as cap charges/discharges. Observe timing |
| 26 | Electromagnet | Wrap copper wire around a bolt → connect battery → it picks up paper clips! Add more coil turns → picks up more! |

---

## UNIT 11 — Button-Controlled RC Car (Sessions 27–29)
*"Real RC control — NO computer, NO code"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 27 | L298N motor driver — what it does, pin layout | Connect one DC motor. Press/release wires manually → motor goes forward/backward |
| 28 | Wire 4 push buttons as F/B/L/R | Button → L298N input pin → motor driver controls direction. Two motors, four buttons |
| 29 | **Build: Button-Controlled RC Car** | Full car assembly: chassis + 2 DC motors + L298N + 4 push buttons + 9V battery. Forward/Backward/Left/Right — all physical buttons, zero Arduino! |

> **WOW factor:** This looks exactly like an RC car. Students drive it with a button panel. No code. Pure electronics wiring logic!

---

## UNIT 12 — High-Level Machines (Sessions 30–33)
*"Big builds, real engineering"*

| Session | Topic | Activity / Challenge |
|---------|-------|---------------------|
| 30 | **Hovercraft** | DC fan motor (large) + lightweight cardboard/styrofoam base + balloon or plastic skirt underneath. Motor creates air cushion → glides on smooth floor! |
| 31 | **Electromagnetic Crane** | Bolt + coil of wire + battery + switch + hanging hook. Switch ON → magnet lifts metal objects. Switch OFF → drops them. Build a crane arm from cardboard |
| 32 | **Bristle-Bot** | Small vibrating motor + toothbrush head (cut bristles angled). Vibration + angled bristles = directional movement! Race them across the table |
| 33 | **Mini Motor Projects** | Teams choose: spinning top (motor + symmetrical disc), boat propeller (motor + submerged prop in tub), or windmill (motor + card blades) |
