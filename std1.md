## 🔗 Chain Project Deep-Dive: The Vibrating Bug

**The Big Idea:** A DC motor with an off-center weight VIBRATES. Put that vibrating thing on legs and it MOVES. No code. No Arduino. Just physics!

**What You Bring Next Year:** Your fully built and decorated Vibrating Bug. In 2nd class, you'll crack it open and upgrade the motor system with an L298N driver and 4 control buttons — turning it into an RC car.

**Build Steps:**

| Step | What You Do |
|------|-------------|
| 1 | Cut a lightweight cardboard oval body (~10cm long) |
| 2 | Hot-glue a small DC motor to the top of the body |
| 3 | Attach a blob of hot glue off-center on the motor shaft (asymmetric weight) |
| 4 | Add 4–6 legs: bent wire or toothbrush bristle pieces, angled slightly forward |
| 5 | Wire: battery → slide switch → motor (all taped to underside of body) |
| 6 | Decorate: paint, googly eyes, pipe cleaner antennae, foam wings |
| 7 | Switch ON → vibration + angled legs = crawling motion! |

**The Physics:** When the off-center weight spins, it creates uneven centrifugal force. The angled legs convert that vibration into forward motion — like a real insect vibrating its feet!

**Keep It Safe:** Put your bug in a zip-lock bag labelled with your name. Store it. In 2nd class — it gets a serious upgrade!

## 🚀 Standalone Problem-Solving Project: Mini Fire Alarm

**The Real-World Problem:** In a fire emergency, people need to be WARNED immediately. A loud alarm + visual warning saves lives.

**Your Solution:** A simple fire alarm that triggers with a button press — simulating what a smoke detector does when it detects fire.

**How It Works:**
- Push button = "fire detected" trigger
- Buzzer = loud alarm sound
- Red LED = visual alert (flashes)

**Components Needed:**

| Component | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Active buzzer | Makes the alarm sound |
| Red LED | Visual alert — lights up |
| 220Ω resistor | Protects the LED |
| Push button | Trigger — simulates fire detection |
| 9V battery + holder | Power source |
| Cardboard box | Alarm housing |

**Build Plan:**
1. Wire button → buzzer + LED in parallel (both activate on button press)
2. Add resistor in series with LED
3. Mount in a small red cardboard box
4. Label: "FIRE ALARM — PRESS IN EMERGENCY"
5. Test: press button → buzzer screams + LED lights. Release → silence.

**Extension Challenge:** Add a second button that makes the alarm STOP (reset). Now it behaves exactly like a real fire alarm panel!

## Year-End Exhibition

Students present TWO items:

1. **The Vibrating Bug** — switch it on, place it on the table, watch it crawl. Explain: what creates vibration? Why does it move forward?
2. **The Mini Fire Alarm** — press the button, the buzzer fires and LED lights. Explain: what is the circuit path? What does each component do?

Visitors get to press the alarm button. Parents love the bug race. It's unforgettable!

**Connection:** The bug motor is the SEED of a multi-year journey. Every year it gets smarter. By 9th class it responds to hand gestures. This is where it all begins.
